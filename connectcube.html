Connect Cube

Maintain a connection to loved ones by sharing a moment.

Overview

For families who live far apart from each other, it’s a constant challenge to maintain an meaningful connection. They must actively make the effort to stay in contact. For our closest relationships, a connection goes beyond active communication. If there was a better way to share moments from a distance, then families could feel like they are together. Connect Cube explores implicit communication with our families while living far apart

  • Process:
    User Research (Surveys, Interviews), Persona, Experience Map, Storyboard, User Flow, Mood board, Wireframes, User Testing, Digital Fabrication, Prototyping
  • Tools:
    Figma, Fusion 360, Javascript, MongoDB, Arduino, Ps, Ai, Google Forms

Define and Discovery

Discovery research

I conducted surveys and interviews to understand the communication behaviors between potential users and their relatives, and gain more knowledge about the need to be connected and which ways they feel that they could enhance that connection.

Define

The surveys and the interviews let me highlight their behaviors and motivations. I focused on the demografic, woman below 45, who mostly communicate with their parents and close relatives on a daily basis to make sure that they are ok. A Persona, Empathy Map and Experience Map was developed from the discovery phase.

User Interviews - Findings

The research was oriented around how people feel about the quality of communication, what other options they could consider and what other ways they can envision communication with loved ones.

Design process

Ideate

Inspired by our current socially distant lives, I searched for a unique interaction to strengthen our connections. A series of sketches helped me visualize the concept and possible interaction models. This process resulted in a set of cubes that, when a person joins them together, send a message to another person.

Storyboard

The story takes place in their homes. They make a connection using the cubes. The storyboard illustrates the casual use of the cubes and how easy it is to share a moment.

User Interaction

The person is invited to join objects together to share their activities with another person. This message consists of an activity they want the other person to know about, an emotion or a feeling. The activity cubes are interchangable.
