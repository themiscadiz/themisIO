Visualizing Hurricane Data

Visual Design | P5*js | CSV Data | Data Visualization

Tools: P5*js, Figma

In this sketch, I visualize different data from hurricane data sets between 2016 and 2017. I explored a CSV data set of Hurricanes of 159 storms samples from 2016 and 2017, originally sourced from from NOAA. I visualized the direction of the storm, distance from land, if the eye of the storm is on land, the strength of winds and length of reading related to the duration of storms. The selection of hurricanes is navigable with a sequence of thin rectangle buttons at the bottom.

Resist! Designing for Food Sovereignty in Puerto Rico

Tech + Activism | UX Research | UX Design

This project intends to strengthen food sovereignty the movement in Puerto Rico.
Constraints of massive importation, poverty, and climate change on the island, generate a need to look for alternatives for a sustainable local economy. With this in mind, I ask, it is possible to design a system that encourages solidarity with local and rural economies?

Through this process I envision a platform where consumers and farmers can have a direct relationship. This direct connection between farmers and their community would improve the visibility of farmers, and shorten the chain of access to locally grown crops.
