Sounds of the Amazon

Data Visualization | Visual Journalism | Visual Design

Team: Themis Garcia, Vanessa Chesnut and Sarah Liriano

Role: Data Visualization

Tools: P5.js, Ai, D3, Flourish, Observable

The Amazon forest supports multiple ecosystem functions and is a huge repository for humankind. The symphony of sounds heard within its habitats can point to the richness of its biodiversity.

This project consists of analyzing acoustic data in order to explore the Biophony of the Amazon. Leveraging sound, we aim to explore which animals live in an ecosystem and compare the frequency range of sounds for each animal and explore biodiversity through a spectrograph analysis. This information will help us develop a story around the habitat and its sonic characteristics.

For the animal comparison, we used the p5.sound library to get the spectral centroid. Spectral Centroid to analyse the main characteristic of a sound in a given snapshot. This technique summarizes the most characteristic frequency per snapshot. To represent the shapes per animal, we organized the spectral centroid of each animal song in use to compare the frequency range of each animal.

Food Sovereignty in Puerto Rico

Tech + Activism | UX Research | UX Design

Tools: Figma

Strengthening the food sovereignty movement in Puerto Rico.
The constraints of importation, poverty, and climate change generate a need to look for alternatives for a sustainable local economy. With this in mind, I ask, it is possible to design a system that encourages solidarity with local and rural economies?

Through research, I discovered opportunities and gained insights from the community.
I envisioned a platform where consumers and farmers can have a direct relationship, which would improve visibility and shorten the supply chain for locally grown crops.
