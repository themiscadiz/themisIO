    Understanding Collective Transportation in Puerto Rico ‚Äî My Research Journey

The
Problem

The shared-taxi system, known as ‚ÄúPisicorre‚Äù (Step-and-Go), is the collective transportation system with the greatest number of routes on the island, serving both the urban and rural areas. However, due to urban development and the general dependency on the privately owned car, the Pisicorre is on the verge of disappearing. The reduction of service hours, elimination of routes and a decrease in the number of drivers also factor in to decrease customer use. Factoring in little to no economic support from the government, the survival of Pisicorre is exclusively dependent on client demand.

Is it possible to stimulate the public transportation system in Puerto Rico with little-to-no investment?

Product
Overview

Pisicorre is a smartphone application which allows passengers to track bus locations in real-time, check arrival and departure times, and plan their trip with the Pisicorre system. This platform supports passenger needs and increases demand for drivers.

Design Process

Discovery research

In the research process, I discovered that the general attitude in Puerto Rico is that public transportation is unreliable. Loss of motivation is most often noted as occurring when clients wait for the bus to arrive or depart. Lack of itineraries and unpredictability were found to be the main reasons why potential users prefer to use other modes of transportation. Secondary research also demonstrates how the lack of options for reliable public transportation contributes to social inequality.

Research Findings

The research highlighted the social needs and lack of resources that many passengers have. I identified a type of passenger that could help improve the system, increase the demand and benefit other users. Those passengers were the ‚Äúoccasional users‚Äù, a younger generation that currently uses the share-taxi system on an infrequent basis. The Journey Map revealed the high and low points of the travel experience, as well as the reasons occasional users are generally dissatisfied with the Pisicorre experience.

Design Strategy

To develop confidence in the system, it is important to establish visibility and communication between passengers and drivers. Knowledge of the status or location of the bus gives the passengers the visibility that they need and encourages trust that the trip will be completed within an acceptable time frame.
This project aims to strengthen the economy and prevent the disappearance of this unique transportation system.

Ideate

Pisicorre is a smartphone application that allows passengers to track bus locations in real-time, check arrival and departure times and plan a trip using the Pisicorre system.

This platform supports passenger needs and increases demand for drivers. To increase trust in the service, a social connection is established through driver profiles. The design leverages users with smartphones and internet connection (a large number of people in Puerto Rico either have internet or already use smartphones). Drivers could increase their customer base by providing more visibility to their service, thus improving the reliability on this form of transportation.

UX Design

The top priority in designing the UI is to establish a visible connection between passengers and drivers. Visibility into the current status and real-time location data gives passengers the information they need in order to commit to using Pisicorre for their transportation..

User Flow

This sequence describes the user flow that a passenger has when using Pisicorre: entering the desired route, arrival at the bus station, bus departure and trip completion.

The user platform has 5 main features

Real-time Tracking for visibility of buses that are already on route.
Seat Availability Monitoring allows users to estimate departure times without setting specific schedules. Increased usage can give the system a level of predictability in terms of waiting time.
Favorite Routes allow users to access their most commonly used routes, speeding up the process.
Service Alerts inform the users of changes in routes, delays and traffic updates.
Feedback System for monitoring the system‚Äôs functionality and stimulating good service. This is a key feature that gives users a sense of contribution, ownership and empowerment.

System Overview

By leveraging users‚Äô GPS enabled smartphones and cloud-based technologies, this system can be implemented with very little investment. There is no need to develop or purchase hardware, other than the smartphones that users already own.

Conclusion

This project envisions new ways to increase the possibilities for social and physical mobility for the population of Puerto Rico. Having used public transportation for many years, I understand the anxiety of depending on a public transportation system that proved to be unreliable. This project cannot be envisioned without a sense of urgency to create a realistic tool which will have social impact. Although there is still a lot of work to do in this Service Design Project, it is easy to imagine the effect that its implementation can have on the Puerto Rico transportation system.
