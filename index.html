Food Sovereignty in Puerto Rico

Tech + Activism | UX Research | UX Design

Tools: Figma

Strengthening the food sovereignty movement in Puerto Rico.
The constraints of importation, poverty, and climate change generate a need to look for alternatives for a sustainable local economy. With this in mind, I ask, it is possible to design a system that encourages solidarity with local and rural economies?

Through research, I discovered opportunities and gained insights from the community.
I envisioned a platform where consumers and farmers can have a direct relationship, which would improve visibility and shorten the supply chain for locally grown crops.

Who am I?

I'm Themis
(Yes, is a greek mythological name. Originally named by my siblings from the 80's cartoon: Space Legend Ulysses 31.)

I am a designer who cares deeply about education, accessibility, and community empowerment.
Masters candidate at ITP NYU 2021.

I hold a Post-Graduate Degree in Usability, UX and Interaction Design from Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, Spain.

As a designer, my work ranges from developing products and services for people with a diversity of disabilities and socio-economic constraints, to designing affordable solar energy kits for climate resilience.

Originally from Puerto Rico, now living in Brooklyn, NY.
